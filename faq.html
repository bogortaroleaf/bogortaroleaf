Product Information

About Beneng Taro Leaves

Beneng taro (Xanthosoma undipes) is a large-leafed taro variety native to the Banten and West Java regions of Indonesia. It is known for its oversized leaves, high nutritional value, and versatility as a raw material for food, animal feed, and herbal applications. The leaves and stems are widely used in traditional Indonesian cuisine and are gaining strong interest in export markets for their functional food potential.
We currently focus on one main product: dried sliced Beneng taro leaves. The leaves are carefully harvested, sliced, and naturally sun-dried to preserve their colour, texture, and nutritional quality. Available in:
  • Retail packaging — 100g resealable ziplock bags
  • Bulk orders — for commercial, food industry, or export purposes
When stored properly in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, our dried sliced Beneng taro leaves can last up to 12 months. Our retail packaging uses resealable ziplock bags to help maintain freshness after opening. For bulk products, airtight storage containers are recommended.
Dried Beneng taro leaves have a wide range of applications, including:
  • Herbal tea and infusions
  • Food ingredient or natural seasoning
  • Animal feed supplement (livestock and poultry)
  • Functional food formulation — high-fibre snacks, powder mixes
  • Traditional herbal and wellness products
  • Research and nutraceutical development

Benefits & Safety

Nutrition & Safety

Beneng taro leaves are a nutritionally rich plant food. They contain:
  • Dietary fibre — supports digestion and gut health
  • Protein — higher than many common leafy vegetables
  • Vitamins — particularly vitamin C and B-complex
  • Minerals — including calcium, iron, and potassium
  • Antioxidant compounds — bioactive phytochemicals
They are actively being researched for potential benefits including blood sugar management, digestive support, and anti-inflammatory properties.
Yes, Beneng taro leaves are safe to consume when properly processed. Raw taro leaves contain calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation if consumed without processing. Our drying method significantly reduces oxalate content. We recommend always cooking or steeping the dried leaves in hot water before consuming — do not eat them raw.
Our processing follows a careful, natural workflow:
  • Harvest — leaves picked at optimal maturity from local Bogor farms
  • Sorting — manual quality inspection and selection
  • Slicing — cut into uniform pieces for consistent drying
  • Sun-drying — natural drying under direct sunlight, no artificial agents
  • Packaging — hygienically packed in resealable ziplock bags
No artificial preservatives or chemical additives are used at any stage.
